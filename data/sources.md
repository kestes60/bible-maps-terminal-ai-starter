## Specific Location Citations

### Gibeon (Josh 10:1-5, 10:12)
**Pleiades ID**: 687900 (Gabaon)
**URL**: https://pleiades.stoa.org/places/687900
**OpenBible URL**: https://www.openbible.info/geo/ancient/aede336/gibeon
**Modern Identification**: el-Jib (Al-Jib), West Bank
**Scholarly Confidence**: >95% (OpenBible: 91.7%)
**Primary Coordinates**: 35.184362°E, 31.849056°N (Pleiades)
**Alternative Coordinates**: 35.183351°E, 31.847451°N (OpenBible)
**Time Periods**: Iron Age Southern Levant (-1200 to -550), Roman (30 BC - AD 640)
**Notes**: Widely accepted identification with high scholarly consensus.

### Jerusalem (Josh 10:1, 10:3)
**Pleiades ID**: 687928 (Ierusalem/Hierosolyma/Col. Aelia Capitolina)
**URL**: https://pleiades.stoa.org/places/687928
**Modern Identification**: Jerusalem, Old City
**Location Precision**: Precise (accuracy: ±5-10m)
**Coordinates**: 35.234156°E, 31.776679°N
**Alternative Names**: Ursalimmu (2nd millennium BC), Hierosolyma (Hellenistic/Roman), Aelia Capitolina (Roman)
**Time Periods**: 2nd millennium BC through modern period
**Notes**: City of David archaeological site represents core settlement.

### Hebron (Josh 10:3, 10:36-37)
**Modern Identification**: Tel Hebron (Tel Rumeida), Hebron, West Bank
**Archaeological Site**: Tell er-Rumeida
**Estimated Coordinates**: 35.108°E, 31.529°N
**Elevation**: 936m above sea level
**Time Periods**: Early Bronze Age III (ca. 2600-2200 BCE) through modern
**References**:
- The Hebron Fund: https://hebronfund.org/tel-hebron-archaeological-park-and-admot-ishay-tel-rumeida-neighborhood/
- Biblical Archaeology: https://biblical-archaeology.org/en/locations/חברון-תל-רומיידה/
**Notes**: Massive Middle Bronze Age II (1750-1550 BCE) city wall documented. Multiple conquest traditions in biblical text.

### Jarmuth (Josh 10:3, 10:23)
**Modern Identification**: Tel Yarmut (Khirbet el-Yarmuk), Israel
**Archaeological Site**: Tel Yarmuth
**Coordinates**: ~34.997°E, 31.717°N (estimated from Palestine Grid 147124)
**Location**: 26km WSW of Jerusalem, Shephelah region
**Elevation**: 405m
**References**:
- BibleWalks: https://www.biblewalks.com/yarmut/
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jarmuth
**Notes**: King Piram led coalition against Gibeon. Now Israeli National Park.

### Lachish (Josh 10:3, 10:31-32)
**Modern Identification**: Tel Lachish (Tell el-Duweir), Israel
**Archaeological Site**: Tel Lachish
**Coordinates**: 34.849°E, 31.565°N (31°33′54″N 34°50′56″E)
**Location**: 40km SW of Jerusalem, Judean lowlands
**Time Periods**: Bronze Age through Iron Age, major destruction 701 BCE
**Excavations**:
- James Leslie Starkey (1932-1938)
- Tel Aviv University (1973-1987)
**References**:
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tel_Lachish
- BibleWalks: https://www.biblewalks.com/lachish/
**Notes**: Major fortified city, extensive archaeological documentation. Israeli National Park.

### Eglon (Josh 10:3, 10:34-35)
**Modern Identification**: Tel 'Eton (Tel Aitun), Israel [PRIMARY]
**Alternative**: Tel el-Hesi [UNCERTAIN]
**Archaeological Site**: Tel 'Eton
**Coordinates**: 34.9284°E, 31.4897°N (31°29′23″N 34°55′42″E)
**Location**: 12km SE of Lachish, 17km SW of Hebron
**Scholarly Confidence**: High for Tel 'Eton (consensus), Low for Tel el-Hesi (outdated)
**Excavations**: Bar Ilan University (Avraham Faust)
**References**:
- BibleWalks: https://www.biblewalks.com/eton/
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eglon_(biblical_place)
- OpenBible: https://www.openbible.info/geo/ancient/a244d8f/eglon
**Notes**: Tel 'Eton third largest site in Judah (after Jerusalem and Lachish). Albright's Tel el-Hesi identification now rejected.

### Debir (Josh 10:38-39)
**Modern Identification**: Khirbet Rabud, West Bank [PRIMARY]
**Alternative**: Tell Beit Mirsim [OUTDATED]
**Coordinates**: 35.017°E, 31.417°N (31°25′N, 35°01′E)
**Location**: 13km SW of Hebron, Judean hill country
**Scholarly Confidence**: High for Khirbet Rabud (current consensus)
**Excavations**: Moshe Kochavi, Tel Aviv University (1968-1969)
**References**:
- Oxford Biblical Studies: http://www.oxfordbiblicalstudies.com/article/opr/t256/e879
- OpenBible: https://www.openbible.info/geo/modern/ma06d55/khirbet-rabud
**Notes**: Only Late Bronze Age city south of Jerusalem. Located near upper and lower water sources (Wells of ῾Alaqa). Albright's Tell Beit Mirsim identification abandoned by most scholars.

### Makkedah (Josh 10:10, 10:16, 10:28)
**Modern Identification**: Khirbet el-Qom [POSSIBLE]
**Alternative**: Khirbet el-Kheishum [POSSIBLE]
**Coordinates**: Uncertain (estimated ~34.95°E, 31.55°N for el-Qom)
**Location**: Between Lachish and Hebron (if el-Qom), 14km W of Hebron
**Scholarly Confidence**: Low - identification uncertain
**References**:
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khirbet_el-Qom
- OpenBible: https://www.openbible.info/geo/modern/m3c0a28/khirbet-el-qom
**Notes**: Cave tradition important (Josh 10:16-27). Idumean ostraca mention "Maqqedah." Multiple proposed locations, no consensus.

### Libnah (Josh 10:29-30)
**Modern Identification**: Tel Burna, Israel [PRIMARY]
**Archaeological Site**: Tel Burna (Tell Bornat)
**Coordinates**: 34.819°E, 31.698°N
**Location**: 35km SW of Jerusalem, Shephelah region along Nahal Guvrin
**Scholarly Confidence**: High (W. Albright, Y. Aharoni, excavation team)
**Excavations**: Ariel University (Itzhaq Shai, Joe Uziel, 2010-2024)
**References**:
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tel_Burna
- Biblical Archaeology Society: https://www.biblicalarchaeology.org/daily/digs-2015/tel-burna-an-introduction-to-the-biblical-town/
- BibleWalks: https://www.biblewalks.com/telburna/
**Notes**: One of 13 Kohanic cities. Late Bronze Age cultic finds support Canaanite occupation. Border between Judah and Philistia.

### Azekah (Josh 10:10-11)
**Modern Identification**: Tel Azekah (Tell Zakariyeh), Israel
**Archaeological Site**: Tel Azekah
**Coordinates**: 34.949°E, 31.701°N
**Location**: 26km NW of Hebron, 20-30km SW of Jerusalem, Shephelah
**Elevation**: 347m above sea level
**Excavations**:
- Frederick J. Bliss & R.A. Stewart Macalister (1897-1900)
- Lautenschläger Azekah Expedition: Oded Lipschits (Tel Aviv) & Manfred Oeming (Heidelberg) (2012-present)
**References**:
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azekah
- BibleWalks: https://www.biblewalks.com/azekah/
- Lautenschläger Expedition: https://azekah.org/
**Notes**: Strategic location in Elah Valley. Scene of David vs Goliath. Destroyed by Sennacherib 701 BCE. LMLK seals found. Israeli National Park (Britannia Park).

## Methodology Notes

1. **Primary Sources**: Pleiades and OpenBible.info selected as primary authoritative sources due to:
   - Peer review and scholarly oversight
   - Open licensing (CC-BY)
   - Continuous updates
   - Geographic precision with uncertainty indicators

2. **Confidence Levels**:
   - **High**: >90% scholarly consensus, extensive archaeological evidence
   - **Moderate**: 70-90% consensus, some competing theories
   - **Low**: <70% consensus, multiple unresolved proposals
   - **Uncertain**: No scholarly consensus, speculative identification

3. **Coordinate Selection**: When multiple coordinate sets available, preference given to:
   - Pleiades (peer-reviewed, standardized methodology)
   - Archaeological excavation reports
   - OpenBible.info (aggregates multiple scholarly sources)

4. **Alternative Identifications**: Documented when:
   - Historically significant (e.g., Albright's proposals)
   - Currently debated in scholarly literature
   - Confidence level requires noting uncertainty
